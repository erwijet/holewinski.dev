## What needs motivate Tony's participation in groups? To what extend are those needs met in his current job?

Tony's Need for Control manifests through his need to have influence in the lives of others. As his family grew up and friends retired, he feels he has less influence. This results in Tony becoming an _autocrat_, where he ends up dominating group settings. Examples of this include his need for strict schedules and unwelcoming attitude towards new, younger styles.

Additionally, Tony is ancillarily motivated by Need for Inclusion, which due to professional alienation and a lack of consistency with his Basketball social group, may not being met to the same extent it once was. This leads Tony to not feel accepted by his younger coworkers, which may exacerbate this _autocratic_ tendencies in hope of _gaining the group's attention_.

## What roles does Tony assume in his groups, and how do these roles affect his attitude and behavior as a group member?

Tony seems to function as many of his groups' Coordinator. He manages priorities, schedules, and tasks. In his professional group, this title is even formalized as _manager_. His attributes also reflect that of a _Shaper_, due to his energetic and argumentative demeanor.

## How Can Tony balance his interaction style and become less aggressive?

Tony needs to learn to balance passivity and aggression. Be demeanor currently reflects a low apprehension, which tends an towards tendencies of group domination and compulsive talking. He can work to curb these tendencies by making a conscious effort to _stop talking_ and listen to those around him, as well as keeping an eye on members who may be more apprehensive to speak, calling them into the conversation if he sees their body language indicate they have something to share.

Tony is dealing with many tensions which could be explained with dialectics. Notably, his inability to be open to changing, last minute plans is indicative of the _structure vs. spontaneity_ dialectic. He also seems to be struggling to juggle the _conflict vs. cohesion_ dialectic.
