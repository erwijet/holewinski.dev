## Ideal Group Size

- Most group members and leaders prefer groups of three to nine members
- Groups larger than nine are generally less productive
- Groups of give to sene members are generally more effective for problem solving discussions
- To avoid tied votes, and odd number of members is usually better than an even number

## Collaboration

- Characterized by coordinated group interaction in which members share a common goal, respect others' perspectives, and contributes, and work together to create a successful group experience

## Interdependence

- Refers to the influence of each group member on the thoughts and actions of other members.
- A successful group with interdependent members functions as a cohesive team in which all members feel responsive for doing their part.

## Working

- They physical and/or mental effort group members expend when trying to accomplish something.

## Common Goal

- Shared purpose or objective toward which a group work is directed
	- Why are you in a meeting?
	- Why should we care or work hard?
	- Where are we going?

> Meetings should have agendas. Need to give people a context of the "common goal" for that hour of a meeting.

## Dialectics

- Method for examining and resolving two contradictory or opposing ideas

## Balancing Group Dialectics

- Individual Goals <--> Group Goals
	- Personal goals are compatible with group goals (we both succeed )
- Conflict <--> Cohesion
	- Cohesive groups are committed, united, and willing to engage in conflict
- Conforming <--> Nonconforming
	- Members value group norms, but are willing to change
- Task Dimensions <--> Social Dimensions
	- Members want to get the job done and also value their social relationships
- Homogenous <--> Heterogeneous
	- Members value member similarities and differences
- Leadership <--> Followership
	- Effective leadership requires loyal and competent followers
- Structure <--> Spontaneity
	- Members recognize the need for structured procedures and the value of creative thinking
- Engaged <--> Disengaged
	- Members expend energy and work hard, but also need to rest.
- Open System <--> Closed System
	- Members welcome input and interchange, but also protect the group and its work

## Ethics

- Understanding of whether groups member's communications behaviors meet agreed upon standards of right and wrong.

## Ethics and Balance

- Do group members agree upon and apply standards of right and wrong to group situations and member interaction?
- Do group members follow the National Communication Association Credo for Ethical Communication?
